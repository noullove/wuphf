# Launch Week Operating Brief

Date: 2026-04-14
Owner: GTM
Status: locked_for_launch_week

## Strategic Call

Keep the `Back Office AI` brand and the current launch wedge.

Do not widen the audience, add a second brand, or lead with a generic "AI productivity" message.

The sharpest launch story is:

**Back Office AI finds the hidden margin buried in boring workflows for founder-led small teams, then rebuilds one system per episode with practical AI they can deploy this week.**

## Channel Wedge

- Brand: `Back Office AI`
- Thesis: `AI Back Office for Small Teams`
- Launch wedge: founder-led service businesses, agencies, and consultancies with `2-20` people
- Core pain words: inbox overload, follow-up drift, meeting sprawl, onboarding chaos, reporting drag, support handoff mess
- Anti-positioning: generic AI news, passive-income framing, hypey "replace your whole team" promises, enterprise theater

## Audience Promise

Every upload should make one promise to the viewer:

**I will show you one boring workflow that is quietly leaking time, follow-up, or margin, then hand you the lightest AI system and proof asset to fix it this week.**

What the viewer should consistently get:

- one workflow, not a grab-bag
- one clear before/after operating change
- one asset they can use immediately
- one opinionated stack recommendation only after the workflow is clear

## CTA Ladder

For the first `6-10` uploads, the cold front door should be the `Hidden Margin Weekly Teardown Checklist`, not the broader starter pack.

| Step | Offer | Job | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| `1` | `Hidden Margin Weekly Teardown Checklist` | capture email from pain-aware viewers | default primary CTA for uploads built around a painful workflow |
| `2` | workflow-specific stack page | monetize buyer intent | use in description and thank-you flow only when the stack is genuinely shown or compared |
| `3` | `AI Back Office Starter Pack` | deepen trust after opt-in | make this the backend upgrade, not the cold front-door asset |
| `4` | `AI Back Office Audit` | convert red-hot workflow pain | mention only after the score-based framework or proof asset has earned it |
| `5` | `Implementation Sprint` | monetize the teams that need done-with-you help | offer only after an audit or explicit buying intent |

CTA discipline:

- one primary CTA per upload
- one fallback CTA only if it naturally supports the episode
- no sponsor slot before proof
- no description that looks like a software directory

## Monetization Order

### Launch-week order

`video -> Hidden Margin Weekly Teardown Checklist -> workflow stack page -> AI Back Office Starter Pack -> Audit`

This is the right launch-week order because it turns vague pain into a diagnosis first, captures email before asking for a bigger commitment, and keeps affiliate links tied to real operator intent.

### What stays off until later

- low-ticket template packs: turn on after upload `10`, once one workflow lane proves repeatable clicks and opt-ins
- implementation sprint: keep behind the audit
- sponsors: wait for repeatable view floors and a clear winning pillar
- ads: last priority, not a launch-week objective

## Factory KPIs

Judge the factory by three numbers, each tied to a different failure mode:

| KPI | Target | What it tells us |
|---|---|---|
| Long-form CTR | `4.5%+` by video `6` | whether titles and thumbnails are opening the door |
| First 30-second retention | `70%+` | whether the hook and workflow promise are strong enough to keep the right viewer |
| View-to-email capture rate | `0.45%+` | whether the content is turning attention into owned demand |

`0.45%` view-to-email capture is the working roll-up target from two underlying guardrails already in the assets:

- description-to-click rate: `1.5%+`
- landing-page opt-in rate: `30%+`
