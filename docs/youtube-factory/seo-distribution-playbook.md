# SEO And Distribution Playbook

Date: 2026-04-14
Owner: GTM

## Purpose

This playbook defines how the channel gets discovered and how each long-form upload is distributed after publish. The strategy is intentionally boring: compound search, browse, and owned-audience traffic around repeatable small-team workflow pain.

## Traffic Thesis

The first 90 days should rely on three loops:

1. **Search loop**
   - Win specific "how do I automate X?" queries with operator-language titles.
   - Build playlists around recurring workflow categories so each episode feeds the next.
2. **Browse loop**
   - Package videos around obvious pain, clear before/after contrast, and role-specific promises.
   - Use bold but grounded thumbnails that show operational tension, not AI clichés.
3. **Owned-audience loop**
   - Convert long-form viewers into email subscribers, repeat viewers, and later product buyers.
   - Use the email list and LinkedIn as reinforcement, not as separate content businesses.

## Search Architecture

### Query families to target

- `replace [task] with AI`
- `automate [workflow]`
- `best AI stack for [job]`
- `AI [role] for [audience]`
- `how to build an AI [system]`

### Preferred keyword framing

Use the pain or workflow first, then the enabling system.

Examples:

- `Automate Client Onboarding With This AI Workflow`
- `The Best AI Stack for Founder-Led Sales Follow-Ups`
- `I Built an AI Inbox Operator for a 5-Person Business`

Avoid leading with vague tool hype like:

- `Best AI Tools 2026`
- `10 ChatGPT Hacks`
- `Make Money With AI`

### Playlist structure

Create playlists that reinforce search intent and suggested-video flow:

- `AI Sales Ops for Small Teams`
- `AI Inbox, Meetings, and Follow-Ups`
- `Client Onboarding Systems`
- `Support and Customer Success Workflows`
- `Reporting and SOP Automation`

## Packaging System

### Title rules

- Lead with the task, role, or broken process.
- Promise one meaningful outcome.
- Keep tool names secondary unless the video is a stack teardown.
- Stay focused on 45-62 characters when possible.

### Thumbnail rules

- 2-4 words max.
- Show contrast: chaos vs system, manual vs automated, scattered vs clean.
- Use operational objects viewers recognize: inboxes, calendars, checklists, dashboards, CRM stages.
- Do not use robot faces, neon circuitry, or fake-money bait.

### First-30-seconds rules

Open with:

1. the broken workflow
2. who is suffering
3. what system is being built
4. the practical result promised by the end

## Distribution Workflow Per Upload

### Before publish

- Confirm the episode has one primary CTA and one fallback CTA.
- Validate title against one search-intent bucket.
- Validate thumbnail against one emotional contrast.
- Link the episode to a playlist before publishing.
- Prepare one LinkedIn post and one email blurb while the episode is still in production.

### Publish day

- Publish long-form video.
- Add pinned comment with the primary CTA and a workflow-request prompt.
- Send the email list a short note: pain, promise, link.
- Publish one LinkedIn post built around the video's core framework, not a transcript dump.

### Days 2-7

- Review CTR, early retention, comment quality, and CTA clicks.
- Swap title or thumbnail if CTR lags but retention is healthy.
- Pull one short clip only if the long-form has early retention or CTA traction.
- Reply to comments with links to the relevant checklist, template, or waitlist.

### Day 30

- Update description links and UTMs if the offer stack changed.
- Add cards, end-screen routing, and playlist links based on what adjacent episode is winning.
- Decide whether the topic graduates into a template pack, audit CTA, or sponsor-friendly proof point.

## Repurposing Rules

### Long-form -> Shorts

Only cut Shorts from videos that have already proven one of these:

- strong first-30-second retention
- unusually high comments
- above-baseline CTA clicks

Best short angles:

- the workflow before/after
- the punchiest teardown insight
- the strongest operating rule

### Long-form -> LinkedIn

Use a single contrarian lesson or framework from the video.

Good pattern:

- broken workflow
- operating rule
- one screenshot or diagram
- CTA back to the full video or starter pack

### Long-form -> Email

Use email to reinforce the business model:

- flagship videos -> drive back to the episode
- template launches -> drive to the product page
- audit-intent videos -> invite replies or audit calls

Do not split attention across multiple equal CTAs in one send.

## Distribution Priorities By Stage

### Stage 1: first 1,000 subscribers

- Search-first titles
- playlist discipline
- every upload feeds a lead magnet
- one LinkedIn post per upload

### Stage 2: once a pillar starts winning

- add 1-2 Shorts from winners only
- publish email recaps weekly
- expand internal linking between related episodes

### Stage 3: once products and sponsors exist

- use high-intent videos to launch template packs
- save sponsor reads for videos with proven audience fit
- keep service CTAs on workflow-fix episodes, not every upload

## Metrics That Matter

Track these together, not in isolation:

- CTR
- first-30-second retention
- average view duration
- description click-through rate
- opt-in rate by video
- affiliate click-to-trial rate
- comment themes by workflow category

The rule: a video only becomes a repeatable content lane if it wins both attention and business intent.
