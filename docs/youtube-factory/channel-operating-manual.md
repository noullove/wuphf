# Channel Operating Manual

Date: 2026-04-14
Owner: GTM

## Channel Thesis

**Working thesis:** `AI Back Office for Small Teams`

The channel exists to show founder-operators how to remove repetitive operational work with practical AI systems, not vague inspiration and not "make money with AI" fluff.

## Niche Rationale

### Why this wedge

- It fits a faceless format cleanly: screen recordings, workflow diagrams, text-driven storytelling, voiceover, and templated visual systems are enough.
- It maps to real buyer pain: inbox overload, meeting sprawl, follow-up drift, reporting drag, onboarding chaos, and support handoff mess.
- It monetizes before ad revenue: the viewer can convert into a checklist download, software trial, paid template pack, or audit call on day one.
- It creates original content safely: original scripts, original diagrams, and original workflow rebuilds are more monetization-safe than clips, compilations, or "AI news" scraping.

### Why the launch wedge is narrower than the brand

The brand can speak to "small teams" broadly, but launch should target **founder-led service businesses and agencies with 2-20 people**.

Why start there:

- fastest pain-to-purchase loop
- easier examples: proposals, onboarding, follow-ups, reporting, support, scheduling
- higher odds of affiliate conversion and service upsell
- lower need for enterprise credibility theater

Expansion can come later into SaaS ops, executive assistants, and internal ops generalists. Launch should stay tight.

## Audience Thesis

### Primary ICP

- founder-led agencies
- consultancies
- small B2B service firms
- fractional operators running lean client-service teams

### Profile

- team size: 2-20
- software spend already exists
- no dedicated ops architect
- too much work still lives in inboxes, docs, calendars, and manual follow-up
- wants execution help, not abstract AI philosophy

### Core jobs to be done

- "Show me one system I can deploy this week."
- "Help me avoid hiring for a broken manual workflow."
- "Make my team look more organized without buying enterprise junk."

### Emotional drivers

- fatigue from constant context switching
- fear of dropping leads or client work
- embarrassment about operational chaos
- desire to look sharp, modern, and in control

## Packaging Rubric

### Title rules

- One workflow per video. No kitchen-sink titles.
- Name a role, task, or operating mess the viewer already recognizes.
- Pair one concrete promise with one curiosity gap.
- Prefer 45-62 characters when possible.
- Use plain business language before jargon.

### Preferred title formulas

- `I Built an AI [Role] for a [Audience]`
- `Replace [Task] With This AI [System]`
- `We Rebuilt [Workflow] in [N] Steps`
- `The Best AI Stack for [Specific Job]`
- `[N] Tasks Every [Audience] Should Automate Before Hiring`

### Avoid in titles

- `make money`
- `passive income`
- `full autopilot`
- `replace your whole team`
- generic year-stamped `best AI tools` listicles

### Thumbnail rules

- 2-4 words max
- one visual idea, not a collage
- title and thumbnail should complement each other instead of repeating line-for-line
- show tension: chaos vs system, manual vs automated, scattered vs clean
- use operational objects: inbox, calendar, CRM pipeline, notes, dashboard, checklist, proposal

### Avoid in thumbnails

- random robot faces
- neon circuitry clichés
- giant ChatGPT logo spam
- red-arrow clutter
- money stacks and fake-y revenue bait

### Hook rules

The first 20 seconds should establish:

1. the broken workflow
2. who is suffering from it
3. the new system being built
4. the concrete result the viewer should expect by the end

## Publishing Cadence

### First 12 weeks

- Weeks 1-4: `2` long-form videos per week
- Weeks 5-8: `2` long-form videos plus `1` short/cutdown from a winning long-form
- Weeks 9-12: `2` long-form videos plus `2` cutdowns, with low-ticket offer CTAs turned on for the winning pillar

### Weekly rhythm

- Monday: publish long-form case-study or rebuild
- Wednesday: score backlog, review comments, update offer routing
- Thursday: publish long-form tool teardown or workflow explainer
- Friday: cut short-form clips from the best-performing long-form

### Cadence rule

Never increase volume before the factory can maintain:

- repeatable packaging quality
- consistent publish payload QA
- at least one strong CTA destination per upload

## CTA Routing Rules

- Role replacement -> starter pack, checklist, or audit waitlist
- Function-specific systems -> checklist or workflow template
- Tool stack teardowns -> affiliate stack page
- Before/after rebuilds -> audit or implementation sprint
- Templates and playbooks -> low-ticket template pack or email capture

Every video gets one primary CTA and one fallback CTA. No description should look like a SaaS flea market.

## Launch KPIs

### Packaging KPIs

- Long-form CTR target: `4.5%+` by video 6
- First 30-second retention target: `70%+`
- Average view duration target: `40%+` on 8-12 minute videos

### Revenue KPIs

- Lead magnet visitor-to-opt-in: `30%+`
- Description-to-click rate: `1.5%+`
- Affiliate click-to-trial rate: category dependent, but track from day one
- Low-ticket conversion target once launched: `1-3%` of qualified landing traffic

### Strategic decision gates

- Double down on the first pillar that wins both CTR and conversion
- Kill topics that get views without clicks or clicks without retention
- Do not add sponsors before at least one pillar has repeatable view floors

## Day-One Non-Negotiables

- Every video must be linked to an approved offer.
- Every publish payload must include disclosure-ready affiliate and sponsorship fields.
- Every workflow shown must feel deployable by a small team inside one week.
- Every asset should sound like an operator built it, not a trend chaser.
