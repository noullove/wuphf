# Hidden Margin Money Path

Date: 2026-04-14
Owner: GTM

## Strategic Call

Keep the current `Back Office AI` wedge.

Do not add a second brand, second audience, or second business model right now.

The sharper revenue story is not "AI for everything." It is "find the hidden margin trapped in boring workflows, then rebuild one system at a time."

That angle matches the current audience:

- founder-led service businesses
- agencies and consultancies with `2-20` people
- small operators already paying for too many tools and still losing time in handoffs

## Channel One-Liner

**Back Office AI finds the hidden margin buried in boring workflows for founder-led small teams.**

Expanded promise:

Back Office AI shows operators where time, follow-up, and handoff slippage are quietly killing margin, then rebuilds one workflow per episode with practical AI systems they can deploy this week.

## Lead Magnet

### Asset

- Name: `Hidden Margin Weekly Teardown Checklist`
- Format: `PDF + Notion scorecard + weekly review prompt`
- Promise: `Score one boring workflow in 10 minutes and see where margin is leaking before you buy more software or hire around the mess.`

### Outline

1. Pick one workflow.
   Use cases: inbox triage, follow-up, onboarding, meeting cleanup, reporting, support handoff.
2. Measure the weekly drag.
   Capture volume, touches, owners, response lag, and rough time spent.
3. Score the margin leaks.
   Score time leak, delay leak, drop-ball risk, rework, and tool sprawl from `1-5`.
4. Mark the human checkpoints.
   Separate what should stay human from what can be drafted, routed, or summarized.
5. Find the highest-value fix.
   Choose the one step that would save the most time or revenue if cleaned up this week.
6. Choose the lightest stack.
   Pick note capture, CRM, docs, automation, or inbox tooling only after the workflow gap is clear.
7. Commit the next `7` days.
   Name owner, build date, review date, and proof metric.
8. Read the score.
   `green`: document and keep going
   `yellow`: tighten handoffs and review loops
   `red`: this workflow is expensive enough to justify an audit or rebuild
